METRICTHRESHOLDWHY IT MATTERSDATA SOURCE
ROE > 12% Measures how efficiently the bank generates profit from shareholders' equity. Below 12% = destroying value. Auto-computed
Loan-to-Deposit (proxy) < 90% How much of deposits are lent out. Too high = liquidity risk. Too low = inefficient capital use. Auto-computed
CET1 Ratio > 10% Core capital buffer vs risk-weighted assets. Regulatory minimum. Canadian banks typically 11-13%. Annual Report / Earnings Release
LCR > 100% Liquidity Coverage Ratio — can the bank survive 30 days of stress? Regulatory minimum is 100%. Annual Report (Liquidity section)
NPL Ratio < 2% Non-performing loans as % of total loans. Rising NPL = credit quality deteriorating. Annual Report (Credit Risk section)
METRICFULL NAMEFORMULA
ROE Return on Equity Net Income ÷ Average Shareholders Equity × 100
Loan-to-Deposit Loan-to-Deposit Ratio (proxy) Total Debt ÷ Total Assets × 100 (proxy — true L/D requires internal data)
CET1 Common Equity Tier 1 Ratio CET1 Capital ÷ Risk-Weighted Assets × 100 (Basel III standard)
LCR Liquidity Coverage Ratio High-Quality Liquid Assets ÷ Net Cash Outflows over 30 days × 100
NPL Non-Performing Loan Ratio Non-Performing Loans ÷ Total Loans × 100

Canadian Big 6 context: BMO, RBC, TD, BNS, CM, NA typically maintain CET1 > 12%, LCR > 130%, NPL < 0.5% due to OSFI oversight. Use these as peer benchmarks.
Step 1 — Find the Annual Report
Go to the bank's Investor Relations page → Annual Reports → Most Recent PDF
Step 2 — Find CET1
Search PDF for "CET1" or "Common Equity Tier 1". Usually in Capital Management section. Canadian banks report quarterly via earnings releases too.
Step 3 — Find LCR
Search for "Liquidity Coverage Ratio" or "LCR". In Liquidity Risk Management section. Also disclosed in quarterly supplemental packages.
Step 4 — Find NPL
Search for "Non-performing" or "Impaired Loans". In Credit Risk section. Usually shown as $ amount — divide by total loans disclosed nearby.
